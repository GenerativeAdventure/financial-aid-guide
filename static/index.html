How US Financial Aid Guide Works

A grounded AI approach to complex regulatory knowledge

The problem with asking ChatGPT about financial aid

General-purpose AI models are trained on internet text — which includes outdated, incorrect, and oversimplified information about student aid. They hallucinate dollar amounts, cite repealed regulations, and can't distinguish between the 2023 and 2025 rules. For something as consequential as financial aid, that's not good enough.

The architecture

Three layers work together on every question you ask.

📄
Source: FSA Handbook The official US Department of Education Federal Student Aid Handbook — 10 volumes, ~490,000 words. The authoritative source for every financial aid rule, eligibility requirement, and dollar limit.
↓
🕸️
Knowledge Graph 224 concepts (nodes) and 200 relationships (edges) extracted from the handbook using AI. Clusters related topics — FAFSA, SAI, Pell Grants, R2T4, Direct Loans — and maps how they connect. Visible in the graph panel.
+
🔍
BM25 Text Search 1,965 chunks of the actual handbook text, indexed for precision keyword retrieval. When you ask a question, the top relevant passages — with exact dollar limits, specific eligibility rules — are pulled directly from the source.
↓
🤖
GPT-5.4 Synthesis The retrieved graph context and handbook passages are handed to OpenAI's GPT-5.4. The model reads the actual source material and synthesizes a clear, conversational answer — grounded in the handbook, not its training data.
↓
💬
Your answer Accurate, specific, and cited. The graph panel highlights which concepts were used to answer your question.

Why a knowledge graph?

🗺️
Structure over chaos

Financial aid rules form a web — FAFSA feeds the SAI, the SAI determines Pell eligibility, Pell affects loan packaging. The graph makes those dependencies explicit and traversable.

🎯
Precision retrieval

Instead of searching 490,000 words blindly, the graph routes the query to the right neighborhood of concepts first. BM25 then finds the exact passages within that scope.

🔗
Cross-volume reasoning

A question about loan limits touches Vol 3 (packaging), Vol 8 (Direct Loans), and the Verification Guide. The graph knows those connections; plain text search doesn't.

The business lens

This pattern — domain document → knowledge graph + text index → grounded LLM — applies to any regulated, high-stakes domain where hallucination is unacceptable:

Healthcare coverage rules Tax code & IRS guidance Legal contracts Insurance policy analysis HR & benefits compliance Building codes Government procurement Clinical trial protocols

The knowledge graph adds a layer that pure RAG misses: it captures how concepts relate, not just where text appears. That's the difference between a search engine and an advisor.
